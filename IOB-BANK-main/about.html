Our Story

Indian Overseas Bank (IOB)

Indian Overseas Bank (IOB) was founded on 10th February 1937 by Shri. M.Ct.M. Chidambaram Chettyar, a pioneer in many fields. The Bank was founded by him with the main objective of specializing in foreign exchange business in banking to take the Bank across the globe

IOB started business simultaneously - at Karaikudi, Chennai and Rangoon in Burma (presently Myanmar) followed by a branch in Penang, Malaysia. At the dawn of Independence IOB had 38 branches in India and 7 branches abroad -and Deposits stood at Rs.6.64 Crores and Advances at Rs.3.23 Crores at that time.

IOB was one of the 14 major banks that were nationalized in 1969. On the eve of Nationalization in 1969, IOB had 195 branches in India with aggregate deposits of Rs.67.70 Cr. and Advances of Rs.44.90 Cr. Bank Presently has its Overseas Presence in 4 Countries Singapore, Hongkong, Thailand and Srilanka.

Milestones in Our Journey

Key moments that shaped Indian Overseas Bank's legacy

1937

Founding Year

Indian Overseas Bank was founded by M. Ct. M. Chidambaram Chettyar with the vision of specializing in foreign exchange and overseas banking.

1969

Nationalization

IOB became one of the 14 major banks that were nationalized by the Government of India to ensure better control and public access to banking services.

2000

Core Banking Initiatives

IOB introduced Core Banking Solutions across branches, enhancing efficiency and providing a seamless banking experience to customers.

2020

Digital Transformation

Accelerated the shift towards digital banking by launching mobile apps, digital savings accounts, and AI-powered customer support.

2024

Recent Progress

IOB reported record profits and enhanced its digital ecosystem, introducing real-time trade finance portals and expanding financial inclusion efforts.
